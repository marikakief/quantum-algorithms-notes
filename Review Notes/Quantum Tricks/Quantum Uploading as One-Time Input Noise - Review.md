# Review: Quantum Uploading as One-Time Input Noise

Source note: [[Quantum Uploading as One-Time Input Noise]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Kannan, Putterman, and Cotler, arXiv:2605.02057, checked current arXiv v1.

## Verdict

Minor issues. This is a good trick card. It should distinguish the abstract input-channel theorem from the concrete transduction hardware assumption even more explicitly.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 12-19: the upload-first pipeline is clear.
- Lines 20-34: good statement of the effective input channel. Keep the distinction between the arbitrary CPTP residual channel and depolarizing channel after twirling.
- Line 36: the conceptual slogan is fine, but it is a little too editorial for a reusable card. A more neutral version would be easier to cite.
- Lines 47-52: the surface-code scaling summary is useful. Add physical-qubit and decoding overhead as "not included in this abstraction."
- Lines 54-58: excellent caveat. This is the key limitation.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add "the separation is upload-first versus raw noisy quantum processing, not quantum versus classical" because this card will otherwise be misused in advantage claims.
