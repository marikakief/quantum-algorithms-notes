# Review: EQA Assessment Framework (State Preparation vs Classical Heuristic)

Source note: [[EQA Assessment Framework (State Preparation vs Classical Heuristic)]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Lee et al., arXiv:2208.02199 / Nature Communications 14, 1952 (2023).

## Verdict

Minor issues. This is a good conceptual card. It just needs firmer language around empirical heuristics and overlap-to-energy implications.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 6-12: the two-axis framing, "classical hardness" plus "preparable overlap", is exactly the right lesson from the EQA paper.
- Line 18: improving local overlap does not generally yield a clean, representation-independent energy-error guarantee unless spectral gaps, variational structure, and normalization are specified. Phrase this as a useful diagnostic rather than a theorem.
- Lines 23-31: the distinction between determinant overlap, CSF overlap, and MPS overlap should be explicit if the card compares numbers across papers.
- Lines 40-45: keep the warning that QPE resource estimates with assumed perfect state preparation are incomplete. That is the central value of the card.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a compact checklist: classical benchmark, overlap witness, state-preparation cost, and QPE/filtering cost. Those are the four ingredients needed before claiming chemistry advantage.
