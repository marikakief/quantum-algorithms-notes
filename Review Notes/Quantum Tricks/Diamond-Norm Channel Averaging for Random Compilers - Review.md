# Review: Diamond-Norm Channel Averaging for Random Compilers

Source note: [[Diamond-Norm Channel Averaging for Random Compilers]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Campbell, arXiv:1811.08017: https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.08017
- Childs, Ostrander, Su randomized product formulas, arXiv:1805.08385: https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.08385

## Verdict

Major issues. The card mixes the simple averaged-channel telescoping argument with the stronger Hastings-Campbell mixing lemma, and it blurs average-channel guarantees with individual random-circuit guarantees.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 11-16: the protocol should be phrased as a bound on the randomized channel obtained by averaging over choices. It does not prove that each sampled circuit is close to the target.
- Line 13: the notation `E_1 circ ... circ E_N` looks like one fixed random sequence. The channel analyzed by qDRIFT is the expectation/mixture over all sampled sequences.
- Line 16: convexity of diamond norm gives a conservative bound, but the stronger mixing lemma is not just this convexity argument.
- Lines 28-33: the Hastings-Campbell lemma is important, but the variables need definitions: `Lambda` should be the channel mixture generated by random unitaries `U_j`, and `V` the target unitary channel.
- Line 33: the Stochastic QSP application is later context. Keep it, but separate it from qDRIFT and product-formula randomization.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Split the card into two sections: "qDRIFT averaged-channel telescoping" and "mixing lemma quadratic improvement." They are related but not the same statement.
