# Tranche 19 Resolution

Resolved Tranche 19: QSP / QSVT / Hamiltonian Simulation Foundations.

## Summary

All Tranche 19 supervisor comments were audited against the current notes. A large fraction had already been implemented before this pass, especially in the sparse-simulation, BCK/BCCKS, LCU-origin, randomized-product-formula, and qDRIFT notes. I made additional edits where normalization, query-complexity, precision-term, or scope language was still too loose.

The main theme was to keep the simulation parameter visible: `t`, `T`, `tau`, `alpha t`, `d ||H||_max t`, and `d^2 ||H||_max t` are not interchangeable. I also made the post-2015 Taylor-vs-QSP distinction explicit: truncated Taylor has multiplicative `tau log(tau/epsilon)/loglog(tau/epsilon)` behavior, while QSP/qubitization has additive `tau + log(1/epsilon)/loglog(1/epsilon)` behavior.
