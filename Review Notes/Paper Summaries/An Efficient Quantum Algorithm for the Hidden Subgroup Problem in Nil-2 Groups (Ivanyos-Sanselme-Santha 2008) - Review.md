# Review: An Efficient Quantum Algorithm for the Hidden Subgroup Problem in Nil-2 Groups (Ivanyos-Sanselme-Santha 2008)

Source note: [[An Efficient Quantum Algorithm for the Hidden Subgroup Problem in Nil-2 Groups (Ivanyos-Sanselme-Santha 2008) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Ivanyos, Sanselme, and Santha, "An efficient quantum algorithm for the hidden subgroup problem in nil-2 groups", arXiv:0707.1260 / LATIN 2008.

## Verdict

Minor issues. The note is clear and correctly presents the nil-2 paper as an extension of the extraspecial-group algorithm. The main improvement is to keep the classical reduction and the quantum hiding procedure separated.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 7-9: good TL;DR. It already identifies the role of Chevalley-Warning.
- Lines 26-34: nil-2 structure is accurate.
- Lines 38-45: the two-layer proof organization is helpful.
- Lines 47-62: classical reduction is dense; a reader would benefit from a bullet explaining why reducing to cyclic order-`p` hidden subgroups is enough.
- Lines 66-80: the exponent-`p` normal form and automorphism equations are the core algebra.
- Lines 84-105: quantum hiding procedure is accurately described.
- Lines 107-122: Chevalley-Warning witness search is well summarized. Mention that the paper needs an efficient constructive version, not just existence.
- Lines 140-145: caveats are good.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a small comparison table with the extraspecial paper: dimension of `G'`, number of copies, and phase-cancellation equations.
