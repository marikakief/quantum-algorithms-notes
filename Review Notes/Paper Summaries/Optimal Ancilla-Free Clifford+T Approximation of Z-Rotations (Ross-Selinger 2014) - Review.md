# Review: Optimal Ancilla-Free Clifford+T Approximation of Z-Rotations (Ross-Selinger 2014)

Source note: [[Optimal Ancilla-Free Clifford+T Approximation of Z-Rotations (Ross-Selinger 2014) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Ross and Selinger, "Optimal ancilla-free Clifford+T approximation of z-rotations", arXiv:1403.2975, v3 2016.

## Verdict

Minor issues. This is a careful and technically mature note. The main needed edits are terminology and a few proof-condition labels.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 35-41: the optimality/near-optimality story is right. Keep the distinction between exact optimality with factoring and near-optimality under Hypothesis 8.3.
- Lines 47-65: the exact-representability setup is clearly explained.
- Lines 101-114: the denominator-exponent/T-count connection is useful. Mention that normal form conventions matter for the `2k-2` versus `2k` distinction.
- Lines 130-150: the norm-equation step is accurate at the right level of detail.
- Lines 184-200: good statement of the heuristic near-optimal theorem. The note should say "prime-distribution hypothesis" every time this result is quoted outside the caveats.
- Lines 262-269: the caveats are excellent.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a small "model box": ancilla-free, deterministic Clifford+T, operator norm, and optional global phase. These assumptions are what make the optimality statement precise.
