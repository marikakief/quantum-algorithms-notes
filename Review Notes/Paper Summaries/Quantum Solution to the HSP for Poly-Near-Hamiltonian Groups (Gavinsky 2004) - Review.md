# Review: Quantum Solution to the HSP for Poly-Near-Hamiltonian Groups (Gavinsky 2004)

Source note: [[Quantum Solution to the HSP for Poly-Near-Hamiltonian Groups (Gavinsky 2004) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Gavinsky, "Quantum solution to the hidden subgroup problem for poly-near-Hamiltonian groups", Quantum Information and Computation 4 (2004).

## Verdict

Minor issues. The normalizer-core method is summarized well. The important qualifier is that the algorithm depends on usable normal-HSP subroutines and subgroup-membership machinery.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 13-20: good definition of `M_G` and `k_G`.
- Lines 28-40: the statement of both index conditions is clear.
- Lines 46-54: the key observation `H triangleleft HM_G` is correctly emphasized.
- Lines 62-76: the quotient/coset notation is useful, though `U_{G/M_G}(X)` should be described as generated by the lifted cosets, not merely a set union.
- Lines 78-91: the coset membership test is the fragile part. Keep the assumptions visible.
- Lines 105-113: random quotient sampling and the doubling argument are well described.
- Lines 119-127: good treatment of coset-intersection checking using subgroup membership.
- Lines 133-135: key results are accurate but conditional; keep the phrase "required normal-HSP calls are efficient."
- Lines 148-154: caveats are excellent.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add one sentence contrasting "Hamiltonian" in group theory with Hamiltonian simulation, since the term can mislead readers outside algebra.
