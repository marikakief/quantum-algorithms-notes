# Review: Consequences and Limits of Nonlocal Strategies (Cleve-Hoyer-Toner-Watrous 2004)

Source note: [[Consequences and Limits of Nonlocal Strategies (Cleve-Hoyer-Toner-Watrous 2004) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Cleve, Høyer, Toner, and Watrous, "Consequences and Limits of Nonlocal Strategies," CCC 2004, arXiv:quant-ph/0404076.

## Verdict

Minor issues. This is a strong conceptual summary. The main improvements are to mark which claims are in the original paper and which are retrospective post-MIP* context.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 9-15: good nonlocal-game definition. Keep "supremum" for the quantum value; finite-dimensional attainability is subtle.
- Lines 21-28: the framework-level contribution is well described. The final sentence about MIP* = RE is retrospective context, not part of the 2004 result.
- Lines 34-51: the four games are summarized accurately and usefully.
- Lines 58-62: the MIP/MIP* motivation is right. The examples show natural oracularization protocols can become unsound with entangled provers; they do not by themselves characterize MIP*.
- Lines 68-84: theorem list is good. For Theorem 10, specify whether "m" counts questions and whether the dimension/qubit statement is exact or up to the Clifford-algebra construction.
- Lines 92-95: comparison table is useful. The MIP* = RE result should be dated and labelled as much later.
- Lines 101-105: caveats are excellent. The erratum/current-version note is important and should stay near the theorem it affects.
- Lines 111-115: reusable ideas are well chosen. The Tsirelson correspondence and Magic Square entries are likely worth separate trick cards if not already present.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a "then versus now" box: 2004 paper results, 2010 corrected version/erratum, and 2020 MIP* = RE context.
