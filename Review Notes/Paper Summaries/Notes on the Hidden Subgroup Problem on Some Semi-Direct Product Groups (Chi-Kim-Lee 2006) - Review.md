# Review: Notes on the Hidden Subgroup Problem on Some Semi-Direct Product Groups (Chi-Kim-Lee 2006)

Source note: [[Notes on the Hidden Subgroup Problem on Some Semi-Direct Product Groups (Chi-Kim-Lee 2006) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Chi, Kim, and Lee, "Notes on the hidden subgroup problem on some semi-direct product groups", arXiv:quant-ph/0604172 / Physics Letters A 359 (2006).

## Verdict

Minor issues. The note is accurate and properly labels the paper as incremental. The factor-splitting hypothesis should remain front and center because it does nearly all the work.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 11-20: good statement of the arithmetic restriction. The condition `p ∤ p_j - 1` is the reason the action cannot spread across other prime-power factors.
- Lines 22-31: good high-level assessment.
- Lines 35-61: the CRT and automorphism-splitting proof outline is clear.
- Lines 63-74: subgroup splitting by coprime direct-product factors is the right group-theoretic fact.
- Lines 78-85: solving the two components is accurate.
- Lines 87-121: appendix summary is useful, but keep it separate from the main theorem.
- Lines 135-138: theorem statement is good. Add that the action must satisfy the paper's nontriviality/restriction assumptions.
- Lines 162-166: caveats are strong and should not be shortened.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a sentence saying this is not a new measurement primitive; it is a decomposition that delegates to abelian HSP and Inui-Le Gall.
