# Review: The Hidden Subgroup Problem Review and Open Problems (Lomont 2004)

Source note: [[The Hidden Subgroup Problem Review and Open Problems (Lomont 2004) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Lomont, "The hidden subgroup problem - review and open problems", arXiv:quant-ph/0411037 (2004).

## Verdict

Minor issues. The note is a useful survey summary and correctly treats Lomont as a map rather than a new algorithm paper. The main improvements are structural: add a title heading and make the 2004 date limitation more prominent.

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 1-5: missing top-level title. Add the usual `# ... — Paper Notes` heading for consistency.
- Lines 7-11: the TL;DR is good. The three diagnostic questions are the most reusable part of the review.
- Lines 22-36: good scope list. It should say explicitly that some results are now historically superseded or sharpened.
- Lines 40-48: abelian HSP summary is accurate.
- Lines 52-65: excellent separation of weak sampling, strong sampling, distinguishability, and reconstruction.
- Lines 69-77: good nonabelian landscape. Keep "efficient QFT is not enough" prominent.
- Lines 89-95: the connections section should be updated where later vault notes now exist, especially Kuperberg/Regev/ISS nil-2.
- Lines 101-106: the caveats are good. Move the "predates..." point into the opening so readers do not treat it as a current survey.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a short "use with care" sentence: this is best for vocabulary and early landscape, not for current constants or post-2004 status.
