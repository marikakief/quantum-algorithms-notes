# Review: On Quantum Algorithms for Noncommutative Hidden Subgroups (Ettinger-Hoyer 1998)

Source note: [[On Quantum Algorithms for Noncommutative Hidden Subgroups (Ettinger-Hoyer 1998) — Paper Notes]]

## Primary Sources Checked

- Ettinger and Høyer, "On quantum algorithms for noncommutative hidden subgroups", arXiv:quant-ph/9807029 (1998).

## Verdict

Minor issues. This is a good compact historical note. The main fix is again structural: add a title heading and keep "query/sample efficient" separate from "time efficient."

## Line-Anchored Comments

- Lines 1-5: missing top-level title.
- Lines 18-24: the dihedral setup is clear.
- Lines 26-30: good assessment. This paper is an information-theoretic precursor, not an efficient DHSP algorithm.
- Lines 34-37: reducing to the reflection case by first finding the rotation part is the right outline.
- Lines 40-47: the cosine/sine-biased distribution is a useful contrast with later labelled-qubit presentations.
- Lines 49-54: emphasize that maximizing over all candidates is exponential in `log N`; this is the computational bottleneck.
- Lines 56-61: key results are accurate.
- Lines 65-73: the historical positioning against EHK, Kuperberg, and Regev is good.

## Missing or Suggested Additions

- Add a short comparison between the biased-sample picture here and the later Fourier-labelled qubit picture used by Kuperberg.
