The note now labels the result as a 2026 v1 streaming-sample / bounded-space machine-size separation, not a blanket practical QML claim; the oracle-sketching formulas are marked as schematic/theorem-specific; readout is scoped to sparse test-query families with shadow-norm dependence; and the hidden application parameters are tabulated.
- `Exponential Speedups in Fault-Tolerant Processing of Quantum Experiments`: accepted the architectural-scope corrections. The note now states upload-first fault-tolerant processing versus raw noisy quantum processing, adds a noise-parameter glossary, defines the paper-specific noisy-oracle class, fixes the corrupted fraction, and warns that deconvolution formulas are version-specific while the variance can grow exponentially.
- `Analyzing Prospects for Quantum Advantage in Topological Data Analysis`: accepted the indexing and classical-baseline feedback. The note now distinguishes `k`-cliques, `(k-1)`-simplices, `H_{k-1}`, and `beta_{k-1}`; narrows clique-enumeration claims to enumerative baselines; adds the Dirac-gap versus Laplacian-gap warning; scopes random-graph speedups to the analyzed Erdos-Renyi window; and softens additive-error claims.
- `Complexity-Theoretic Limitations on Quantum Algorithms for TDA`: accepted the precision feedback. The note now explains the zero-vs-positive reason multiplicative approximation is hard, narrows the uniform-simplex oracle claim, and separates worst-case exact/nonzero hardness from positive approximate normalized spectral-density regimes.
- `Towards Quantum Advantage via Topological Data Analysis`: accepted the DQC1-scope feedback. LLSD hardness is now explicitly for general sparse/log-local matrices, not a proof for clique-complex ABNE; rank-estimation speedup is described as complexity-theoretic evidence under the DQC1 assumption and the paper's input model; and the dequantization-shield language is narrowed.
- `Information-Theoretic Bounds on Quantum Advantage in ML`: accepted the model-split feedback. The note now foregrounds query/sample rather than runtime, neutralizes the authorial phrasing, marks the restricted classical learner as information-theoretic, specifies the output-system parameter `m`, and adds the classical-shadows compatibility caveat.
- `Learning quantum states prepared by shallow circuits in polynomial time`: accepted the access-model feedback. The note now says the learner is classical with measurement access to copies, defines the region-size parameter before the theorem formulas, preserves the fixed-geometry assumption for quasipolynomial depth, and compares the replacement process to the earlier band-slicing consistency issue.
- `Learning shallow quantum circuits`: accepted the algebra correction. The local sewing identity now includes the normalized local swap `S_i = 1/2 sum_P P_i tensor P_i`, the global identity tracks the system/ancilla registers, the arbitrary-architecture sample theorem is separated from geometric depth refinements, and the log-depth lower bound is scoped "in general."
- `Learning to predict arbitrary quantum processes`: accepted the major scope and formatting feedback. The corrupted `bigl` control character is fixed, the input stabilizer set is repaired, a top-level scope warning says this is not full process tomography, and the theorem conditions now emphasize locally flat inputs and bounded-degree observables.
- `Classical shadows`: accepted the stub criticism. The note now gives the measurement channel, inverse-channel estimator, unbiasedness, median-of-means role, and shadow-norm sample theorem, replacing the overbroad `O(log M / epsilon^2)` claim.
- `Efficient estimation of Pauli observables by derandomization`: accepted the notation feedback. The hit relation is defined explicitly, the confidence function is explained as a union-bound objective, and a small overlapping-Pauli example was added. The original "no worse than average random schedule, not globally optimal" claim was already correct and kept.
- `Nearly Optimal Quantum Algorithm for Estimating Multiple Expectation Values`: accepted the resource-accounting feedback. The note now moves the state-preparation query caveat into the problem statement, clarifies use of `U_psi` and `U_psi^\dagger`, fixes a ket typo, adds a resource-split table, and avoids reusing an unchecked evenly-spaced-time formula.
- `Matchgate Shadows for Fermionic Quantum Simulation`: accepted the target/overlap distinctions. The note now separates expectations, fidelities, and transition amplitudes; explains the non-Hermitian Slater-overlap estimator via real/imaginary parts or embedding; fixes table typos; and narrows the later two-copy comparison.

## Trick Cards

- `Quantum Oracle Sketching` and `Interferometric Classical Shadow`: accepted the access/readout caveats. The cards now explicitly say oracle sketching is not qRAM, mark phase/sample expressions as model-dependent, and attach the interferometric shadow `log(m)` scaling to bounded shadow norm plus efficient stabilizer-overlap post-processing.
- `Quantum Uploading as One-Time Input Noise`, `Heisenberg Learning Tree Lower Bounds`, `Permutation-Observable Noise Deconvolution for Multi-Copy Moments`, and `Upload-Then-Randomize for Noisy Shadows`: accepted the noisy-processing feedback. The cards now distinguish abstract input-channel theorems from transduction assumptions, add the adjoint-channel view, state that deconvolution is classical post-processing rather than error correction, and scope the shallow-shadow ratio to the paper's contiguous-support small-noise setup.
- `Spectral Density Estimation as Dequantization Shield` and `DQC1-Hardness via Histogram Approximation of Low-Lying Eigenvalues`: accepted the general-matrix versus clique-Laplacian caveat. The cards now define normalized sub-trace estimation and say DQC1-hardness blocks broad generic dequantization for general sparse/log-local matrices, not structure-specific algorithms for clique complexes.
- `Average-Case vs Worst-Case QML Advantage Test`, `Holevo Information Bottleneck for Learning Lower Bounds`, and `Bell Difference Sampling for Magnitude Estimation`: accepted the learning-model clarifications. The cards now include computational-assumption separations as a third category, distinguish Holevo bottlenecks from the matching restricted-classical learner, and say Bell sampling gives magnitudes only, not signed shadows.
- `Quantum Bohnenblust--Hille Inequality for Local Observables` and `Thresholded Low-Degree Heisenberg Learning`: accepted the bounded-degree / locally-flat assumptions. The cards now warn that effective `l1` control is not for arbitrary overlapping `k`-local Hamiltonians, constants can grow with `k`, and the process-learning cost is quasi-polynomial in precision.
- `Projected Quantum Kernel via Local Observables` and `Shadow Kernel for Nonlinear Phase Classification`: accepted the overclaim feedback. The projected-kernel card now treats all-order shadows as expressive but resource-dependent, and says projection need not preserve the original hard feature. The shadow-kernel card now states shot complexity, correlation-length limitations, and its distinction from projected quantum kernels.
- `Quantum-Aided Compressed Sensing for Sparse State Readout`: accepted the tomography caveats. The card now says support measurement finds high-probability support rather than amplitudes, compressed sensing requires rank/sparsity and measurement-ensemble assumptions, and the `M'^2` saving is exponential only when `M'` is much smaller than `M`.

No supervisor suggestion in this tranche was rejected outright. The main intervention was narrowing claims to the exact resource metric or input model: bounded-space streaming, upload-first versus raw noisy quantum processing, general sparse matrices versus clique-complex Laplacians, average-case prediction versus worst-case property prediction, and shadow-norm-dependent readout.
