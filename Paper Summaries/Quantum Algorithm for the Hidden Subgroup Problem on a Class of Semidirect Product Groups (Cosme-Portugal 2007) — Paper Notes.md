# Quantum Algorithm for the Hidden Subgroup Problem on a Class of Semidirect Product Groups (Cosme-Portugal 2007) — Paper Notes

> **Source:** C. M. M. Cosme and Renato Portugal, *Quantum algorithms for the hidden subgroup problem on a class of semidirect product groups*, arXiv:quant-ph/0703223 (2007)
> **Links:** [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0703223)
> **Tags:** #hidden-subgroup-problem #nonabelian #semidirect-product #fourier-sampling #group-decomposition

---

## The computational problem

Given an oracle $f:G\to X$ that is constant and distinct on left cosets of a hidden subgroup $H\leq G$, find generators for $H$.

This paper studies semidirect products

$$
G=\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes_\phi \mathbb{Z}_{p^2},
$$

where $p$ is an odd prime and $r>4$. It also extends the same reduction style to

$$
\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes_\phi \mathbb{Z}_{p^2}
$$

when $N=\prod_i p_i^{r_i}$ and $p\nmid p_i-1$ for each prime factor $p_i$.

The group is generated by $x=(1,0)$ and $y=(0,1)$. The action is determined by

$$
\alpha=\phi(1)(1)\in \mathbb{Z}_{p^r}^{*},
$$

and for $r>4$ the nontrivial choices fall into two isomorphism classes:

- **Class (1):** $\alpha=1+p^{r-2}$.
- **Class (2):** $\alpha=1+p^{r-1}$.

---

## What the paper does

Cosme--Portugal extend the semidirect-product HSP line from $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_p$ to the acting group $\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$. The solution is not a new general nonabelian measurement. It is a case analysis driven by subgroup classification:

1. Classify all subgroups of $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$.
2. Use abelian HSP calls on $\langle x\rangle$ and $\langle y\rangle$ to find
   $H_x=H\cap\langle x\rangle=\langle x^{p^m}\rangle$ and
   $H_y=H\cap\langle y\rangle=\langle y^{p^n}\rangle$.
3. Use $(m,n)$ to decide whether $H$ is cyclic or noncyclic and to remove many subgroup parameters.
4. For the few remaining small-parameter cases, use a two-register Fourier-sampling test that recovers a slope $t$ from a relation $at+b=0\pmod p$ or $\pmod {p^2}$.
5. Alternatively, in the hard-looking leftover cases, observe that the candidate hidden subgroups are normal in a solvable $p$-group, so the normal-subgroup algorithm of Ivanyos--Magniez--Santha reduces the task to abelian HSP.
6. For $\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$, split off the coprime cyclic factors using the same CRT idea as [[Notes on the Hidden Subgroup Problem on Some Semi-Direct Product Groups (Chi-Kim-Lee 2006) — Paper Notes|Chi--Kim--Lee (2006)]].

## Group and subgroup structure

Elements multiply as

$$
(x^{a_1}y^{b_1})(x^{a_2}y^{b_2})=x^{a_1+a_2\alpha^{b_1}}y^{b_1+b_2}.
$$

The subgroups are listed in three forms:

1. Cyclic subgroups

$$
\langle x^{t p^i}y^{p^j}\rangle,
$$

where $0\leq i\leq r$, $0\leq j\leq 1$, and $t\in\mathbb{Z}_{p^\ell}^{*}$ with $\ell=\min\{r-i,2-j\}$.

2. Product-type subgroups

$$
\langle x^{p^i},y^{p^j}\rangle,
$$

where $0\leq i<r$ and $0\leq j\leq 1$.

3. Mixed two-generator subgroups

$$
\langle x^{t p^i}y,x^{p^{i+1}}\rangle,
$$

where $0\leq i<r$ and $t\in\mathbb{Z}_p^{*}$.

This three-parameter description is too large to search directly, so the algorithm first extracts $m$ and $n$ through abelian restrictions.

---

## Algorithm sketch

### Step 1: abelian restrictions

Restrict $f$ to the abelian subgroups $\langle x\rangle\cong\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}$ and $\langle y\rangle\cong\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$. Abelian HSP gives

$$
H_x=\langle x^{p^m}\rangle,\qquad H_y=\langle y^{p^n}\rangle.
$$

If $m=r$ or $n=2$, the hidden subgroup is cyclic. If $0\leq m<r$ and $0\leq n<2$, the hidden subgroup is noncyclic.

### Step 2: use larger abelian subgroups when enough

For many cases in class (1), restricting to

$$
\langle x^{p^2},y\rangle
$$

fully determines the remaining subgroup parameters. This covers all cyclic cases except $m=1,2,3$ in one branch, and all noncyclic cases except $m=1,2$ when $n=1$.

### Step 3: slope recovery for the leftover cases

The remaining cases reduce to distinguishing a cyclic/product subgroup from a tilted subgroup with an unknown slope $t$.

For example, when the possible cyclic hidden subgroups are

$$
\langle x^p\rangle\quad\text{or}\quad \langle x^t y^p\rangle,
$$

prepare

$$
\frac{1}{\sqrt{p^3}}\sum_{a'=0}^{p-1}\sum_{b'=0}^{p^2-1}|a'\rangle|b'\rangle|f(x^{a'}y^{b'})\rangle.
$$

In the tilted case, measuring the oracle register leaves a line state

$$
\frac{1}{\sqrt p}\sum_{\ell=0}^{p-1}|a_0+t\ell\bmod p\rangle|b_0+p\ell\rangle.
$$

Applying $F_p\otimes F_{p^2}$ gives samples satisfying

$$
at+b\equiv 0\pmod p.
$$

If $a\not\equiv0\pmod p$, output

$$
t=-a^{-1}b\pmod p,
$$

then verify the candidate by querying whether $f(x^t y^p)=f(1)$. One run succeeds with probability $1-1/p$.

For the $m=2,3$ cyclic cases the same method uses $F_{p^2}\otimes F_{p^2}$ or $F_{p^3}\otimes F_{p^2}$ and checks both a modulo-$p^2$ candidate and a modulo-$p$ candidate. The noncyclic $m=1,2$ cases use the same line-state idea to choose between

$$
\langle x^p,y^p\rangle\quad\text{and}\quad \langle x^t y,x^p\rangle
$$

or their $m=2$ analogues.

### Step 4: normal-subgroup fallback

The paper notes that all subgroups for which the direct slope test is used are normal in $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ in class (1). For these classified normal candidates, known normal-HSP machinery for the relevant solvable $p$-group reduces the task to abelian HSP. This is not a claim that arbitrary HSP over solvable groups is solved.

### Step 5: class (2)

Class (2), with $\alpha=1+p^{r-1}$, is easier. The subgroup $\langle y^p\rangle$ is normal and the quotient is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_p$. After $m,n$ are known, the hidden subgroup is either already determined, lies inside $\langle x^p,y\rangle$ and is found by abelian HSP, or is normal and is handled by the solvable normal-HSP algorithm.

### Step 6: the $\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ extension

If $N=\prod_i p_i^{r_i}$ and $p\nmid p_i-1$ for all $i$, then the nontrivial $\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ action can only live on the $p$-power component. Thus

$$
\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes_\phi \mathbb{Z}_{p^2}
\cong
\prod_{i\neq 1}\mathbb{Z}_{p_i^{r_i}}\times(\mathbb{Z}_{p^{r_1}}\rtimes_\psi\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}).
$$

The factors have coprime orders, so the HSP splits into abelian HSPs on the cyclic factors and the $\mathbb{Z}_{p^{r_1}}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ instance solved above.

## Key results

- Efficient quantum algorithm for the HSP on $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes_\phi\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ for odd $p$ and $r>4$.
- Overall complexity bounded by

$$
O(\operatorname{poly}((r+2)\log p)).
$$

- Success probability is at least a constant and can be boosted by repetition.
- The algorithm can be viewed either as a direct Fourier-line test in the leftover cases or as a reduction to abelian HSP through normal hidden subgroups in solvable groups.
- The same factor-splitting argument solves restricted $\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ instances.

| Case from abelian restrictions | Consequence |
|---|---|
| `m = r` or `n = 2` | cyclic subgroup family; most cases fixed by larger abelian restrictions, small `m` cases use slope tests |
| `0 <= m < r` and `0 <= n < 2` | noncyclic subgroup family; most cases fixed by restrictions, `m=1,2` leftovers use line-state tests or normal-HSP fallback |
| class (2), quotient by `<y^p>` | reduces to the Inui--Le Gall $\mathbb Z_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb Z_p$-style case plus abelian/normal cases |

## Why it matters

This paper is best read as a follow-on to the semidirect-product chain:

- [[From Optimal Measurement to Efficient Quantum Algorithms for the HSP over Semidirect Product Groups (Bacon-Childs-van Dam 2005) — Paper Notes|Bacon--Childs--van Dam (2005)]] give a broad PGM/matrix-sum lens for $A\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_p$.
- Chi--Kim--Lee use coprime factor splitting for $\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_p$.
- Cosme--Portugal lift the same style to $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$ and restricted $\mathbb{Z}_N\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$.

The conceptual move is: subgroup classification plus a few abelian restrictions can shrink a nonabelian HSP down to one or two finite-field slope tests.

---

## Limits / caveats

- The result depends heavily on the subgroup classification for $\mathbb{Z}_{p^r}\rtimes\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$.
- The condition $r>4$ is built into the automorphism classification used by the paper.
- The $\mathbb{Z}_N$ extension needs the arithmetic restriction $p\nmid p_i-1$; otherwise the action can be nontrivial on other prime-power factors.
- The paper does not address the dihedral HSP or symmetric-group HSP.
- The method is algorithmic for a narrow family, rather than a general nonabelian-HSP framework.

---

## Reusable ideas

1. **[[Modulo-p Slope Extraction for Semidirect-Product HSP]]:** Once subgroup classification leaves one slope parameter $t$, prepare a line state, Fourier sample it, solve $at+b=0$, and verify the candidate by an oracle query.
2. **[[Normal-Subgroup Fallback for Solvable HSP]]:** If the only unresolved candidates are known by classification to be normal inside this solvable $p$-group, switch from a custom slope test to the normal hidden-subgroup algorithm.
3. **[[Coprime-Factor Splitting for Semidirect-Product HSP]]:** The $\mathbb{Z}_N$ extension uses the same CRT splitting idea as Chi--Kim--Lee, now with an acting group $\mathbb{Z}_{p^2}$.
