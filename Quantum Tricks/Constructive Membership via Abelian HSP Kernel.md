# Constructive Membership via Abelian HSP Kernel

## Pattern

To decide whether an element $g$ lies in an abelian subgroup generated by commuting elements $h_1,\dots,h_r$, turn the question into an abelian HSP kernel problem.

Let $s_i=\operatorname{ord}(h_i)$ and $s=\operatorname{ord}(g)$. Define

$$
\varphi:\mathbb Z_{s_1}\times\cdots\times\mathbb Z_{s_r}\times\mathbb Z_s\to G
$$

by

$$
\varphi(\alpha_1,\dots,\alpha_r,\alpha)=h_1^{\alpha_1}\cdots h_r^{\alpha_r}g^{-\alpha}.
$$

The kernel is found by the abelian HSP. It contains an element with last coordinate coprime to $s$ iff $g\in\langle h_1,\dots,h_r\rangle$; such a kernel vector gives a straight-line expression for $g$ in the $h_i$.

## Why it helps

Constructive membership is a standard obstacle in black-box group algorithms. Quantum order finding plus abelian HSP turns this obstacle into a tractable subroutine in unique-encoding settings.

## Where it appears

- [[Efficient Quantum Algorithms for Some Instances of the Non-Abelian HSP (Ivanyos-Magniez-Santha 2001) — Paper Notes]] — quantum implementation of Beals--Babai black-box group tasks.
- [[Efficient Quantum Algorithms for the HSP over Semi-Direct Product Groups (Inui-Le Gall 2007) — Paper Notes]] — uses abelian membership/HSP subroutines in black-box semidirect products.

## Reuse checklist

- The relevant generators commute.
- Orders of all elements can be found.
- The encoding is suitable for evaluating the homomorphism coherently.
- The kernel output can be converted into an explicit membership expression.
