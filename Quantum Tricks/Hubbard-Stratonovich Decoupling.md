## Idea

Rewrite a quadratic operator exponential such as
$$
e^{-tP^2}
$$
as a Gaussian average over linear-phase evolutions generated by \(P\).

## Why it matters

This turns a problem involving \(P^2\) into one involving \(P\) itself, which can then be handled via [[Linear Combination of Unitaries (LCU)|LCU]] or similar decompositions. Algorithmically, that can reduce the effective evolution scale from order \(t\) to order \(\sqrt{t}\), which is exactly why the trick can produce a quadratic-style improvement in condition-number dependence in the right setting.

## When to use it

Use this when:
- the target transform contains Gaussian damping in \(P^2\),
- you have better access to evolutions under \(P\) than under \(P^2\),
- or a matrix-function construction naturally produces heat-kernel-like terms.

## Caveat

The trick is only valuable if the Gaussian integral can be discretized cleanly and the resulting weighted sum does not introduce unbearable coefficient overhead.
