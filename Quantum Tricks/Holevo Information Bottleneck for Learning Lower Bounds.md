# Holevo Information Bottleneck for Learning Lower Bounds

> **Source:** Huang, Kueng, Preskill, arXiv:2101.02464
> **Tags:** #trick #information-theory #learning-theory #lower-bounds

## What it does

Turns a learning lower bound into an information-transmission bound: if a learner could distinguish too many possible quantum processes from too few queries, it would extract more classical information than Holevo's theorem permits.

## The trick

Build a large packing of candidate quantum processes $\{\mathcal E_s\}$ whose induced target functions

$$f_s(x)=\operatorname{tr}(O\mathcal E_s(|x\rangle\!\langle x|))$$

are well separated under the input distribution $D$.

If a learner outputs a hypothesis with small average error, then it can identify which packed process $s$ was used, up to a small ambiguity. That converts the learning task into a communication protocol:

1. Alice chooses $s$ and gives the learner query access to $\mathcal E_s$.
2. The learner queries the process $N$ times.
3. The learner's final hypothesis lets Bob recover information about $s$.

Holevo's theorem bounds the accessible classical information in the quantum systems produced by those $N$ queries. If the packing contains too many well-separated functions, $N$ must be large.

## When to reach for it

Use it for query/sample lower bounds in learning problems where the hypothesis class is generated by quantum states or channels, and where the learner's task can be rephrased as identifying one member of a well-separated packing.

## Complexity

The bound is problem-dependent: the size of the packing gives the information demand; Holevo bounds the information supply by the number and dimension of quantum systems queried.

In Huang--Kueng--Preskill, this machinery is one part of the average-case result that a quantum learner's query advantage over restricted classical learning is at most polynomial. The paper also constructs the restricted classical learner; the Holevo argument explains why a much larger information-theoretic separation cannot occur in that model.

## Caveat

Holevo controls information, not computation. It does not by itself prove that the matching classical learner is computationally efficient.
