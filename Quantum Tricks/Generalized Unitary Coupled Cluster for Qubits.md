## What it does

Extends unitary coupled cluster (UCC) from fermionic systems to general qubit Hamiltonians by replacing fermionic excitation operators with Pauli-based anti-Hermitian generators. Also shows that second-order UCC with relaxed Trotterization is a universal gate set.

## The trick

Standard UCC for fermions: $|\Psi\rangle = e^{T - T^\dagger}|\Phi_{\mathrm{ref}}\rangle$ with $T = \sum_{i<j} t_{ij} a_i^\dagger a_j + \cdots$

**Qubit generalization:** Replace fermionic excitation operators with the full basis of anti-Hermitian qubit operators:

- Single-qubit generators: $i\sigma_p^x$, $i\sigma_p^y$, $i\sigma_p^z$ (generate $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ rotations on qubit $p$)
- Two-qubit generators: $i\sigma_p^\alpha \sigma_q^\beta$ for $\alpha, \beta \in \{x, y, z\}$ (generate entangling operations)
- Higher-order: $k$-body Pauli products

The ansatz becomes:

$$|\Psi(\vec\theta)\rangle = e^{\sum_k \theta_k G_k} |\Phi_R\rangle$$

where $G_k$ are anti-Hermitian operators (products of Pauli matrices with an $i$ factor) and $|\Phi_R\rangle$ is an arbitrary reference state.

**Multi-reference extension:** The reference state $|\Phi_R\rangle$ need not be a computational basis state. It can be an entangled state prepared by adiabatic evolution or another method. The qubit UCC generators then explore the neighbourhood of this reference in a basis-independent way.

**Universality:** Even restricting to single- and double-qubit generators (second-order UCC), the group generated by $\{e^{i\theta \sigma_p^\alpha}, e^{i\theta \sigma_p^\alpha \sigma_q^\beta}\}$ for all qubits $p, q$ and Paulis $\alpha, \beta$ is $\mathrm{SU}(2^n)$ â€” the full unitary group. So UCCSD (singles and doubles) with Trotterization is a universal gate set. The depth needed for universality can be large, but the expressibility is guaranteed.

## When to reach for it

- VQE on spin systems, combinatorial optimization, or any non-fermionic qubit Hamiltonian where the standard chemistry UCC doesn't apply
- When the reference state is unknown or entangled (e.g., output of an adiabatic preparation step)
- As a theoretically complete ansatz family when you need universality guarantees

## Complexity

Gate count per Trotter step: $O(n^2)$ for singles and doubles on $n$ qubits (each generator maps to a [[Pauli-String Exponential via Parity-CNOT Ladder|Pauli-string exponential]]). Total depth depends on the number of Trotter steps $r$ needed: $O(r n^2)$.

## Caveat

Universality doesn't mean efficiency. The number of parameters in UCCSD scales as $O(n^2)$ (singles) + $O(n^4)$ (doubles), and the classical optimization landscape can have barren plateaus for deep circuits ([[Entanglement-Induced Barren Plateaus (Ortiz Marrero-KieferovĂˇ-Wiebe 2021) â€” Paper Notes|Ortiz Marrero et al. 2021]]). Trotterizing $e^{T-T^\dagger}$ to sufficient accuracy adds circuit depth.
