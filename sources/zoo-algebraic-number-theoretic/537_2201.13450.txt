achieving subexponential approximation factor
Lior Eldar‚ąó

Abstract
We give a quantum algorithm for solving the Bounded Distance Decoding (BDD)
problem with a subexponential approximation factor on a class of integer lattices.
The quantum algorithm uses a well-known but challenging-to-use quantum state on
lattices as a type of approximate quantum eigenvector to randomly self-reduce the
BDD instance to a random BDD instance which is solvable classically. The running
time of the quantum algorithm is polynomial for one range of approximation factors
and subexponential time for a second range of approximation factors.
The subclass of lattices we study has a natural description in terms of the lattice‚Äôs
periodicity and Ô¨Ānite abelian group rank. This view makes for a clean quantum algo-
rithm in terms of Ô¨Ānite abelian groups, uses very relatively little from lattice theory,
and suggests exploring approximation algorithms for lattice problems in parameters
other than dimension alone.
A talk on this paper sparked many lively discussions and resulted in a new classical
algorithm matching part of our result. We leave it as a challenge to give a classcial
algorithm matching the general case.
